'Deadpool 3' Breaks Record For Most-Viewed Trailer In First 24 Hours

By The Daily T's Staff | Feb 13, 2024

The first trailer for the much-anticipated and strike-delayed “Deadpool and Wolverine” movie starring Ryan Reynolds and Hugh Jackman was viewed by 365 million people in the first 24 hours it was available, Variety first reported Tuesday, smashing the record to become the most-viewed trailer in one day of all time. The “Deadpool and Wolverine” trailer premiered during the Super Bowl and was promoted by its stars in the aftermath, including an Instagram post from Reynolds that saw the star posing next to the trailer paused on a TV with the caption, “Has everyone seen the #Deadpool trailer? Also has anyone seen my wife?”—a joke about Blake Lively’s much-publicized trip to the big game with friend Taylor Swift.

The hype for the movie—which was originally supposed to be released last September but was pushed to this July—proved to be real as the trailer's views surpassed those of "Spider-Man: No Way Home," which held the previous 24-hour record of 355.5 million. The accolade isn't a new one for Marvel—with Deadpool in the top spot, six of the top-10 most viewed trailers in the first 24 hours belong to movies in the Marvel Cinematic Universe, according to IMDB. "Spider-Man: No Way Home” is now the second-place record holder, followed by the teaser trailer for "Avengers: Endgame" with 289 million views, the official trailer for "Avengers: Endgame" with 268 million and the teaser trailer for Prime Video's "The Lord of the Rings: The Rings of Power" is in fifth place with 257 million. The list of the top 10 most-viewed trailers in the first 24 hours is rounded out by the "Transformers: Rise of the Beasts" teaser with 238 million views, the "Avengers: Infinity War" teaser trailer with 230 million, the live action "Lion King" teaser = got 224.6 million views, the teaser for "Thor: Love and Thunder" had 209 million and, in 10th place, the teaser for the film adaptation of Stephen King's "It" was viewed by 197 million.

"Deadpool 3" was officially announced in September of 2022 and was expected to be released two years later, but it was pushed back to November of 2024, then May of 2025 and is now finally confirmed to be releasing July 26, 2024. The film will see Deadpool offered a spot in the legendary Avengers lineup and features a tie-in with the Time Variance Authority from the Disney show "Loki." The film is the only movie in Disney's Marvel Cinematic Universe to be released next year after the studio's release schedule was turned upside down by last year's actors and writers strikes. As of now, only 5 superhero movies will be released this year: "Madame Web" will hit theaters Wednesday, followed by two more Sony productions in "Kraven the Hunter" and "Venom 3." DC will also release "Joker: Folie à Deux" in October. After “Deadpool,” the next release in Disney’s Avengers-based Marvel world will be "Captain America: Brave New World," slated for a Valentine's Day release in 2025.

The R-Rated "Deadpool" movies are among the most loved Marvel films of recent years by critics and audiences. The first installation has an 85% critics score on Rotten Tomatoes and a 90% audience score, and "Deadpool 2" has an 84% critics score and was liked by 85% of audiences. Those critics scores are higher than any Marvel Studios film released in the last two years. $156.8 million. That's how much the first two Deadpool movies made at the worldwide box office.
